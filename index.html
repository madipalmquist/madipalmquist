Hispanic Population by State

Posted by Madisyn Palmquist on June 20, 2025

This map identifies the density of the Hispanic population across the contiguous United States with darker shades of blue indicating a higher Hispanic population in a given state and lighter shades indicating a lower population in that state. The key problem I wanted to understand through the creation of this map was learning how to make choropleth and proportional symbol maps using U.S. Census data at the national scale. Additionally, I wanted to address questions about where Census data comes from, how Census data is collected, and what the limitations of Census data are. I used the Symbology tool to change the maps to reflect certain information from the Census. In particular, I used it to display a proportional symbol map of the Hispanic population in each state and a choropleth map of the percentage of the Hispanic population in each state. I used the Layout tool to place these maps and edit their appearances, including activating the maps to zoom in on them.
